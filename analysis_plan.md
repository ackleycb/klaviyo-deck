## Slide 2: Methodology

- **Headline:** Research Methodology & Respondent Profile
- **Goals:** Understand how B2C marketers plan, execute, and measure cultural moment marketing activations — with a focus on the 2026 FIFA World Cup, AI adoption, speed-to-market, and channel coordination.

- **Target:** B2C marketers with decision-making authority or influence over lifecycle marketing/CRM strategy
- **Respondent Profile:**
  - Countries: US (n=404), UK (n=435), France (n=213), Germany (n=207), Spain (n=205), Italy (n=200), Australia (n=203), Singapore (n=205)

  - Region: APAC (n=408), EMEA (n=1,260), US (n=404)

## Slide 10: Q7 — Ownership of Cultural Moment Marketing
- **Headline:** Only 27% of organizations have a dedicated cultural marketing team, while 25% have no clear ownership at all

**Key insights:**

2. **VP or Head of Marketing respondents are 54% more likely to say the broader marketing team owns cultural moments** compared to the overall average (40% vs. 26% overall)

## Slide 11: Q8 — Cultural Moment Activation in Past 12 Months
- **Headline:** Nearly half (48%) of marketers activated around both planned and reactive cultural moments in the past year
- **Question:** In the past 12 months, has your team intentionally built marketing activations around cultural moments? For this survey, we distinguish between two types of cultural moments: PLANNED moments (major sporting events, award shows, fashion weeks, awareness days - events you can see coming and prepare for) and REACTIVE moments (viral social trends, unexpected celebrity or influencer moments, breaking news, sudden cultural flashpoints - events that happen fast and demand speed).
- **Chart:** Horizontal bar chart
- **Data:** Yes, around both planned and reactive moments 48%, Yes, mainly around planned moments (e.g. sporting events, tentpoles) 31%, No, we haven't activated around cultural moments 11%, Yes, mainly around reactive or viral moments 10%

**Key insights:**

1. **Apparel/fashion brands are 25% more likely to have activated around both planned and reactive moments** compared to the overall average (60% vs. 48% overall)

2. **Enterprise companies are 17% more likely to have activated around both planned and reactive moments** than the overall average (56% vs. 48% overall)

3. **Both B2B and B2C companies are 13% more likely to have activated around both moment types** compared to the overall average (54% vs. 48% overall)

4. **Founders/CEOs are 2.1x more likely to report no cultural moment activations** compared to the overall average (23% vs. 11% overall)

5. **APAC respondents are 15% more likely to have activated around both planned and reactive moments** compared to the overall average (55% vs. 48% overall)

6. **Health and sporting goods brands are 39% more likely than average to have focused mainly on planned moments** (43% vs. 31% overall)
